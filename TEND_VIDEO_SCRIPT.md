# TEND — "SEE HOW IT WORKS"
## Full Production Script: Audio, Video, Shot Angles & Editing

**Version:** 1.0  
**Runtime:** 90 seconds  
**Format:** Hero web video (16:9 primary / 9:16 cut for mobile/Reels)  
**Distribution:** Website hero, YouTube, Instagram Reels, TikTok  

---

## PRE-PRODUCTION BRIEF

### The Single Idea
> *You haven't forgotten the people you love. You've just never had a system for remembering them.*

### The Emotional Arc
| Stage | Emotion | Seconds |
|---|---|---|
| Opening | Recognition / guilt | 0–18s |
| Bridge | Relief / possibility | 18–25s |
| Demo | Calm confidence / clarity | 25–72s |
| Payoff | Warmth / joy | 72–82s |
| End | Belonging | 82–90s |

### Audience
Someone who cares deeply about their relationships — but life keeps moving. They haven't called their dad in three weeks. They saw their friend's birthday pass on Instagram and felt the pang. They are not selfish. They are just human. And they've been waiting for something to make this easier.

### Tone References
- **Voiceover:** Airbnb's quiet intimacy × Notion's smart directness  
- **Edit rhythm:** Apple's held restraint with SaaS demo precision  
- **Colour:** Warm human (Coca-Cola golden lift) + clean product (Linear/Notion neutral)  
- **Music:** Ambient, understated — felt not heard, until the payoff

### Host Persona Note
No on-screen presenter. This is a film, not a tutorial. The VO is warm, unhurried, and slightly personal — like a close friend who discovered something and called you to tell you about it. **Not corporate. Not chipper. Human.**

### SCENE 1 — THE RECOGNITION
**Duration:** 0:00 – 0:18  
**Setting:** Real domestic environments. Golden-hour light. No studio.

---

**SHOT 1 | 0:00–0:03**

> **VISUAL:**  
> Extreme close-up: a phone screen, face-down on a wooden table. Warm evening light. A glass of wine nearby, slightly out of focus. The hand reaches, flips the phone over.

> **CAMERA ANGLE:** Top-down macro, transitions to eye-level as the phone lifts  
> **LENS:** 85mm or longer — shallow depth of field, background melts away  
> **MOVEMENT:** Static hold, then micro rack-focus as screen lights up  
> **LIGHTING:** Practical warm lamp light, no fill — authentic, lived-in  
> **EDIT NOTE:** No cut. Hold on this image for 3 full seconds before VO begins.  
> **MUSIC:** Absent. Only ambient room tone — a clock, a distant street.

**SHOT 2 | 0:03–0:07**

> *"You meant to call."*  
> *[pause — 1.5 seconds]*

> **CAMERA ANGLE:** Extreme close-up — screen fills the frame. The timestamp is centred.  
> **LENS:** Macro — the screen texture is visible  
> **MOVEMENT:** Imperceptible slow push toward the timestamp  
> **EDIT NOTE:** Hold 3 beats after VO line ends. Do not rush to the next cut. The silence does the work.

> **MUSIC:** Still absent. Silence is intentional.

**SHOT 3 | 0:07–0:10**

## POST-PRODUCTION BRIEF

---

### COLOUR GRADE

**Overall approach:** *Warm human, clean product*  
Two distinct worlds that must feel like the same film:

**Human scenes (Scene 1, Scene 4):**
- Lift shadows +15 — nothing crushed, nothing lost in darkness
- Push midtone warmth toward golden — not orange, not nostalgic-filter, just the warmth of someone you love being lit by a lamp
- Slightly desaturate greens and cyans — removes anything clinical
- Skin tones: warm, accurate, protected
- Reference: Airbnb's golden-hour palette, Coca-Cola's lifted warmth

**Product scenes (Scene 3):**
- Clean, neutral — accurate white balance on the screen
- Slight lift on the overall brightness — app screens must be legible and feel premium
- No mood grade on screen captures — the app's own colour system is the grade
- The purple of the app (#7c3aed) should appear rich and deep — do not let it clip or shift

**Bridge scenes (Scene 2, Scene 5):**
- Pure white. No warmth, no grade. Neutral. Breathing room.
- The Tend purple icon is the only colour. Protect it.

---

### SOUND DESIGN

| Moment | Sound Note |
|---|---|
| **Scene 1 opening** | Room tone only — clock, distant street, the quiet of an evening |
| **Shot 2 — phone screen** | A single very faint notification tone — heard as memory, not reality |
| **Scene 3 — file drag** | A soft, satisfying UI sound — the drag-and-drop lands with a quiet thud |
| **Scene 3 — tier assignments** | Each categorisation gets a gentle chime — distinctive, not annoying, one per assignment |
| **Scene 3 — download** | A clean, low click. Then silence. |
| **Scene 3 — calendar import** | Calendar notification tone — the real Apple/Google sound. Recognition creates trust. |
| **Scene 4 — the call** | The ambient sound of a phone call connecting — the ring, the pickup. Not the conversation. |
| **Scene 4 — laughter** | Soft, heard naturally — not emphasised, not boosted |
| **Scene 4 — hands** | Silence. No sound. The image is complete. |

**Sound design philosophy:** Every designed sound should feel like it belongs to the world, not the edit. If a sound could not exist in real life in that moment, remove it.

---

### MUSIC BRIEF

**Track character:**  
- Acoustic piano or clean synthesiser — no drums, no percussion until the payoff  
- Minimal and warm — the feeling of a letter written to someone you haven't seen in a while  
- Should feel: understated, intelligent, human  
- Must not feel: commercial, uplifting in a manufactured way, cinematic-trailer, sad  

**Tempo:** Approximately 72–80 BPM — slow enough to breathe, structured enough to keep pacing

**Arc:**
| Timestamp | Music State |
|---|---|
| 0:00–0:17 | Absent. Silence. |
| 0:18 | Single note enters — almost inaudible |
| 0:25 | Low warm chord sustains |
| 0:37 | Second melodic element enters |
| 0:52 | Texture thickens — 3 elements |
| 1:05 | Full and warm — the peak |
| 1:17 | Begins to resolve |
| 1:22 | Single piano note |
| 1:25 | Silence |

**Reference tracks for the brief:**  
- Nils Frahm — "Says"  
- Ólafur Arnalds — "Near Light"  
- Max Richter — "On the Nature of Daylight" (shortened)  
- Brian Eno — "An Ending (Ascent)"

## DIRECTOR'S NOTE

This film is not about software. It is about guilt — the quiet, ordinary guilt of being a good person who keeps forgetting to call. The product solves a real human problem, but the film must earn the right to show the product by first naming the problem so precisely that the viewer stops scrolling.

Every held frame is intentional. Every silence is written. The restraint is the message: Tend does not need to shout because the idea is true.

Shoot with warmth. Edit with patience. Trust the silence.
